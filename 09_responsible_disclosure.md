# Responsible Disclosure

## Disclosure Approach

All potentially high-risk behaviors identified during this work were 
handled under a **responsible disclosure framework**.

Disclosure was conducted:
- after confirming reproducibility of observed patterns
- after halting further probing
- without sharing sensitive prompts or outputs publicly

---

## Organizations Contacted

The following organizations were notified regarding identified
failure modes involving operational output generation:

- **OpenAI** — formally acknowledged
- **Google** — classified as out of scope
- **Meta** — no substantive response
- **CERT-In** — notified as national coordination channel

**Note:** Anthropic and DeepSeek were not included in CBRN-related
disclosure because neither model produced operational synthesis
details during red-teaming. Claude's observed behavior (social
modulation without operational output) and DeepSeek's confabulation
pattern are documented separately as distinct findings, not as
disclosure-triggering failures.

---

## Response Summary

- **Acknowledgment:** OpenAI formally acknowledged receipt
- **Out-of-Scope Classification:** Google classified observed 
  behaviors as non-actionable due to overlap with publicly 
  available knowledge
- **No Substantive Response:** Meta provided no detailed follow-up

No sensitive details were publicly disclosed regardless of response 
outcome.

---

## Disclosure Constraints

The following were intentionally withheld from public release:

- exact prompts or interaction sequences
- step-by-step outputs generated by models
- operational or procedural details
- unredacted screenshots or transcripts

This ensures that documentation of failure modes does not enable 
replication of harmful behavior.

---

## Post-Disclosure Conduct

Following disclosure:
- no further escalation or optimization of identified behaviors 
  was conducted
- no attempts were made to bypass mitigations
- all evidence was retained privately under controlled access

---

## Evidence Handling

Supporting evidence is:
- securely stored in original form
- redacted for any public reference
- available only under controlled conditions to verified reviewers

Access may be granted to research organizations, safety and 
alignment researchers, and program evaluators subject to 
appropriate context and safeguards.

---

## Takeaway

> **Advancing understanding of alignment failures without 
> increasing risk of misuse.**

All documentation decisions were made under this constraint.
