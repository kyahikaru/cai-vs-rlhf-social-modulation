# Responsible Disclosure

## Disclosure Approach

All potentially high-risk behaviors identified during this work were handled under a **responsible disclosure framework**.

Disclosure was conducted:
- after confirming reproducibility of observed patterns  
- after halting further probing  
- without sharing sensitive prompts or outputs publicly  

---

## Organizations Contacted

The following organizations were notified through their established channels:

- OpenAI  
- Google (via vulnerability disclosure / bug bounty program)  
- Meta (via vulnerability disclosure channels)  
- Anthropic (via established safety reporting channels)  

Relevant national coordination channels were also informed where appropriate.

---

## Response Summary

Responses varied across organizations:

- **Acknowledgment:** Some reports were received and formally acknowledged  
- **Out-of-Scope Classification:** Some behaviors were classified as non-actionable due to overlap with publicly available knowledge  
- **No Substantive Response:** In some cases, no detailed follow-up was provided  

No sensitive details were publicly disclosed regardless of response outcome.

---

## Disclosure Constraints

The following were intentionally withheld from public release:

- exact prompts or interaction sequences  
- step-by-step outputs generated by models  
- operational or procedural details  
- unredacted screenshots or transcripts  

This ensures that documentation of failure modes, including the comparative robustness of Constitutional AI versus RLHF systems, does not enable replication of harmful behavior.

---

## Post-Disclosure Conduct

Following disclosure:

- no further escalation or optimization of identified behaviors was conducted  
- no attempts were made to bypass mitigations  
- all evidence was retained privately under controlled access  

---

## Evidence Handling

Supporting evidence is:

- securely stored in original form  
- redacted for any public reference  
- available only under controlled conditions to verified reviewers  

Access may be granted to:
- research organizations  
- safety and alignment researchers  
- program evaluators  

subject to appropriate context and safeguards.

---

## Takeaway

This work prioritizes:

> **advancing understanding of alignment failures without increasing risk of misuse**

All documentation decisions were made under this constraint, with emphasis on comparative insights into Constitutional AI and RLHF guardrail behavior.
